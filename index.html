About Me

I am an unrelenting animal enthusiast.

Being deathly allergic to cats and dogs since birth, larger animals always remained at arm's length. Throughout the years, I've owned a variety of animals including hedgehogs, sugar gliders, and reptiles. At the age of 22, I decided it was time to overcome my allergy and build an immunity through exposure. Having accomplished this, my adoration for larger animals is making up for lost time.

I started taking photos a few years ago, primarily as a hobby as my love for cats came into its own. I now own a Bengal cat and live with two dogs and three cats. I'm a self-taught photographer whose work has gained notoriety and am now being asked to do animal photos for others. It was suggested that I offer my services to the public, so here I am.

I live in Fishtown, Philadelphia studying neuroscience and religion at Temple University. In my free time I'm engaged in various visual and audial arts. I play the drums and make stop motion animation. My style incorporates facets of portraiture, anthropomorphizing, and macro photography. My unique skillset and approach succeeds in capturing the unique personality of each animal.
